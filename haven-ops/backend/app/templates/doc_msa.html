Provider and Client are each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

1. Services; Statements of Work

Provider will perform the services described in one or more Statements of Work referencing this Agreement (each an "SOW"). Each SOW is incorporated into this Agreement on execution. If an SOW conflicts with this Agreement, the SOW controls for that engagement only. Provider tracks each engagement under a unique code (e.g., {{ e.code }}); every document issued under an engagement is versioned and frozen at issue in Provider's records.

2. Fees; Invoicing; Payment

Client will pay the fees stated in the applicable SOW. Unless the SOW states otherwise: (a) invoices are due on receipt; (b) amounts unpaid thirty (30) days after invoice may accrue interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less; (c) fees are exclusive of taxes, which are Client's responsibility (excluding taxes on Provider's income); and (d) pre-approved, documented out-of-pocket expenses are reimbursable at cost.

3. Term; Termination

This Agreement runs from the Effective Date until terminated. Either Party may terminate: (a) for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice, in which case any SOW then in progress continues under this Agreement until completed or wound down by mutual agreement; or (b) for material breach not cured within ten (10) days of written notice. On any termination, Client pays for all services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination.

4. Intellectual Property

  1. Pre-existing IP. Each Party retains all rights in materials it owned or developed outside this Agreement.
  2. Client Deliverables. Upon Provider's receipt of full payment under an SOW, Provider assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in the client-specific deliverables identified in that SOW.
  3. Provider Frameworks. Reusable software, libraries, tooling, templates, and know-how that Provider develops or uses across engagements ("Provider Frameworks") remain Provider's sole property. To the extent Provider Frameworks are embedded in a deliverable, Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use them as part of that deliverable.
  4. Open source. Deliverables may incorporate open-source components identified in the SOW or its documentation, licensed under their own terms.

5. Confidentiality

Each Party will protect the other's non-public information received under this Agreement with at least reasonable care, use it only to perform under this Agreement, and disclose it only to those with a need to know who are bound by comparable obligations. This Section does not apply to information that is or becomes public through no fault of the recipient, was rightfully known or received without duty of confidentiality, or is independently developed. If the Parties have executed a separate non-disclosure agreement, its terms control over this Section. These obligations survive termination for three (3) years; trade secrets remain protected for as long as they qualify as such.

6. Independent Contractor

Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, agency, partnership, or joint-venture relationship, and neither Party may bind the other.

EXCEPT AS STATED IN THIS SECTION, SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" AND PROVIDER DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER AN SOW IS CAPPED AT THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THAT SOW IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS BEFORE THE CLAIM AROSE. THESE LIMITS DO NOT APPLY TO BREACHES OF SECTION 5 (CONFIDENTIALITY), A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

9. Indemnification

Each Party will defend and indemnify the other against third-party claims to the extent arising from the indemnifying Party's material breach of this Agreement, negligence, or willful misconduct, provided the indemnified Party gives prompt notice and reasonable cooperation.
